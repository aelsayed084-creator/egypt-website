These are my favourite Egyptian foods!

Koshary

Koshary is Egypt's popular national street food, a hearty vegan dish featuring layers of rice, lentils, pasta, and chickpeas, topped with a tangy tomato sauce and crispy fried onions. It is a comfort food with Indian, Italian, and Egyptian influences, deriving its name from the Hindu dish khichri. The dish is highly customizable, with people choosing their preferred amounts of garlic vinegar, spicy hot sauce, and other toppings, making it a universal and beloved meal across Egypt.

Mahshi

Mahshi is an Arabic word meaning "stuffed" and refers to a category of Middle Eastern dishes where various vegetables or leafy greens are filled with a mixture of rice, herbs, spices, and sometimes meat, then cooked in broth or sauce. Common ingredients for mahshi include zucchini, eggplant, bell peppers, and grape or cabbage leaves, with variations in the stuffing, such as the addition of spices like parsley, dill, and cinnamon or minced meat for non-vegetarian versions.

Fool we Ta'amia

"Fool we ta'amia" is an authentic Egyptian breakfast dish consisting of fool (slow-cooked fava beans) and ta'ameya (the original Egyptian falafel made from fava beans), typically served with baladi bread and fresh vegetables for dipping and making sandwiches. Fool is a flavorful bean paste seasoned with cumin, chili, and oil, while ta'ameya is a green, herb-filled patty that is deep-fried to a fluffy texture.
